Why I stand out

Training that's recognised far beyond Australia.

In Australia, beauty accreditation isn't compulsory. In Ireland, the UK and most countries worldwide, it's essential — and many trainers here don't offer it.

As a Beauty Industry Approval (BIA) accredited educator, my students graduate with an internationally recognised certificate. That's what allows you to obtain insurance and work professionally anywhere in the world — not just locally.

  1. 01

    International recognition

    Trained to a standard recognised in Ireland, the UK and globally — not limited to working only in Australia.

  2. 02

    Insurance ready

    An accredited certificate is what insurance providers require overseas. You graduate with the qualification you need.

  3. 03

    A pathway, not just a certificate

    Whether you plan to travel, move overseas or one day teach yourself, this is the credibility that opens those doors.

Why I teach

I care deeply about good training

When I teach, I want you to leave feeling clearer, steadier and far more confident in what you're doing.

My own career was shaped by good guidance. Representing Ireland at WorldSkills taught me discipline, and having a trainer who really believed in me changed everything.

That is the standard I bring into my academy now in Sydney: careful teaching, real technique and support that is actually useful once you're back with clients.

What your day looks like

An honest run-through of how the training day flows. Times shift slightly on the day depending on the group.
